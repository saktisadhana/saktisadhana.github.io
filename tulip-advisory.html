Package    tulip-pm  (client-side artifact resolver)
Affected   <= 1.3.37         Patched   none
Weakness   CWE-22  Improper Limitation of a Pathname to a
                   Restricted Directory ('Path Traversal')

SUMMARY
  tulip-pm.resolve(pkg) joins caller-controlled `pkg` onto the
  cache base and normalizes it with NO traversal guard:

      resolve(pkg)  ->  normalize("artifacts/cache/" + pkg)

  Artifacts are supposed to stay under   artifacts/cache/ .
  The build secret lives OUTSIDE that tree, at:

      .tulip/build.secret

  A crafted `pkg` can climb out of the cache and resolve straight
  onto the secret artifact, disclosing it to any caller.

PROOF OF CONCEPT
  The vulnerable resolver is the registry's debug interface:

      {registry}/resolve        (registry = window.TulipPM.registry)

  It is gated behind a header. Craft a request whose `pkg` traversal
  escapes artifacts/cache/ onto .tulip/build.secret (Burp / curl):

      GET  {registry}/resolve?pkg=<payload>  HTTP/1.1
      X-Tulip-Debug: 1

  The preview below normalizes your payload locally so you can confirm
  it lands on .tulip/build.secret before firing at the registry.
